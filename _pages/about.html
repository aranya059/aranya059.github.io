I am a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Texas at San Antonio, advised by Dr. Kevin Desai in the Vision & Immersive Realities Lab.

My research centers on vision-language models for clinical applications, medical image segmentation, and explainable, trustworthy AI for healthcare. My recent work spans grounded radiology report generation, longitudinal change analysis in chest X-rays, and the reliability of medical vision-language models — including the grounding-sycophancy tradeoff that governs whether such models stay faithful to image evidence. I am also interested in data-efficient learning, using semi-supervised methods and synthetic-to-real domain alignment to reduce annotation costs in medical imaging. A list of my publications can be found here.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a software engineer for 2.5 years, building full-stack applications and designing system architectures at Nascenia Ltd and RedDot Digital Ltd.

I received my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (KUET) in 2020.

Research Interests: Medical Image Segmentation, Vision-Language Models for Clinical Applications, Radiology Report Generation, Longitudinal Change Analysis, Explainable AI, Semi-Supervised Learning, Synthetic-to-Real Domain Adaptation
